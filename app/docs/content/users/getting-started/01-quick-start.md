## What You Need

A modern web browser is enough to start. Spacewave also has desktop builds for macOS, Windows, and Linux when you want local filesystem storage.

## Create Your Account

1. Open Spacewave in your browser or launch the desktop app.
2. Click **Get Started**.
3. Choose **Sign in or create account** for a cloud-backed account, or **Continue locally** to keep everything on this device.
4. For a cloud account, pick a username and password. Your credentials are encrypted on your device before anything is sent.
5. You land on your dashboard, ready to go.

A cloud account makes it easy to sync between devices and keeps an encrypted backup. A local account keeps everything on the current device with zero network dependency. You can add a cloud account later if you start locally.

## Create Your First Space

Your dashboard has quickstart buttons for the release-ready setups:

- **Drive** gives you a file browser for storing and organizing files.
- **Git Repository** starts a new repository or clones an existing one.
- **Canvas** creates a visual workspace for arranging objects.
- **Empty Space** starts with a blank container.

Click one and your space is created immediately. It opens with the matching viewer and any setup flow needed for that space type.

## Find Your Way Around

The **dashboard** is home base. It lists all your spaces and lets you create new ones.

When you open a space, the **space view** shows your content. What you see depends on the space type: a file browser for Drive, a repository viewer for Git, or a freeform surface for Canvas.

The **bottom bar** shows your open spaces. Click any space there to jump to it, or tap its settings to manage plugins, browse objects, and change space settings.

## What to Try Next

- Drop some files into a Drive space using upload or drag-and-drop.
- Link a second device to see your data sync automatically.
- Check out the Spaces and Files & Data sections for detailed guides.
- Developers: see Plugin Development to build your own extensions.
