## What is a Space

A space is your private container for a project, a topic, or a collection of data. Each space has its own storage, settings, and tools. You can have a Drive space for work files, a Git repository space for code, and a Canvas space for visual planning, all running independently. Spaces sync across your linked devices automatically.

## Creating a New Space

From your dashboard, click any quickstart button to create a space:

- **Drive** gives you file storage with a built-in file browser.
- **Git Repository** starts a repository setup flow.
- **Canvas** gives you a visual workspace for arranging items.
- **Empty Space** creates a blank container.

You can add more objects from the object browser after the space is created.

## Space Settings

Click your space in the bottom bar, then select settings. From here you can rename the space, see what plugins are installed, and browse the objects stored inside.

The space name is just a label for your convenience. Renaming it updates the name on all your synced devices. The underlying space and its data are not affected by a name change.

## Inviting Collaborators

Collaboration starts with device linking. Every device linked to your account can access your spaces. Organization-owned spaces are covered in the Organizations section.

## Deleting a Space

Deleting a space removes it from the current device. If other devices have a copy, those copies stay until you delete them separately. Deleted data is removed from local storage and cannot be recovered, so make sure you have what you need before deleting.
