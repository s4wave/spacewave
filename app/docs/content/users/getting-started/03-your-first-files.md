## Opening the File Browser

Create a Drive space from the dashboard, or open one you already have. The file browser shows everything stored in the space. The toolbar at the top gives you navigation controls, and the path bar shows where you are in the folder tree.

## Uploading Files

Click the upload button or drag files right into the browser window. A progress bar in the bottom bar tracks the upload. Your files are saved to local storage and encrypted automatically. The original file names and folder structure are preserved.

There is no file size limit. Large files are handled efficiently because Spacewave breaks them into pieces and only syncs the pieces that change. Upload speed depends on your device's storage performance, not your internet connection, because files are saved locally first.

## Creating Folders

Right-click in the file browser or use the toolbar to create a new folder. You can nest folders as deep as you want, just like a regular filesystem. Move files around by dragging them or using cut and paste.

## Downloading and Previewing

Click a file to preview it directly in Spacewave. Common formats display right in the interface. To save a copy to your device's downloads folder, right-click and select download.

Files in a Drive space are automatically available on every device linked to your account. Upload a file on your laptop and it shows up on your phone. No manual sharing step required.

## Syncing Across Devices

Sync happens in the background with no action needed from you. When you change a file, only the parts that actually changed get sent to your other devices. This keeps things fast, even with large files.

If you edit the same file on two devices while offline, both versions are kept when they reconnect. The most recent edit becomes the active version. The entire sync process is encrypted, so your file contents are never visible to anyone or anything between your devices.
