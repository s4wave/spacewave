## What This Is

An organization is a shared account that groups spaces under a single owner. Instead of keeping every space under your personal account, you can create an organization, invite other people, and manage all those spaces from one dashboard. Organizations are useful when a team or family needs shared ownership of data.

## How It Works

Open an organization from your dashboard to see its management view. The dashboard shows three things at a glance: the spaces owned by the organization, the current members, and the invite controls.

The **Spaces** section lists every space belonging to the organization. Click any space to jump directly into it.

The **Members** section shows who has access. Each member has a role: **Owner** or **Member**. There is exactly one owner (the person who created the organization). Owners can rename the organization, invite or remove members, manage billing, transfer spaces, and delete the organization. Members can view and work in the organization's spaces but cannot change membership or settings.

The **Invites** section (visible only to the owner) lets you create invite codes. Share a code with someone and they can join the organization. You can revoke a code at any time. Each invite shows how many times it has been used.

From the dashboard header, owners see a **Settings** button. Members who are not owners see a **Leave** button instead.

## Why It Matters

Organizations let you separate personal spaces from shared ones. Spaces owned by an organization stay with the organization even if you transfer them. This makes it easy to hand off projects or collaborate without mixing personal and team data.
