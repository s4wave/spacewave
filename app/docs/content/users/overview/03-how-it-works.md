## Sign Up or Sign In

When you first open Spacewave you pick how to get started:

- **Cloud account.** Your account is backed up to encrypted cloud storage. This is the easiest way to link new devices later and the safest if you lose a device.
- **Local-only account.** Everything stays on this device. No network dependency, no backup unless you add one later.

Either way, your credentials are encrypted on your device before anything crosses the network. You can always add a cloud account later if you started local.

## Open or Create a Space

After signing in you land on the dashboard. The dashboard shows every space you have and lets you create a new one.

Pick a template (Drive, Git Repository, Canvas, or Empty Space) and the new space appears with the right viewer or setup flow. Opening any space loads from your device's storage, not from a server, so it opens fast, even offline.

## Add Files, Notes, Messages

Drop a file into a Drive, clone a repository, or arrange objects on a Canvas. Each change saves to your local storage first, encrypts the new data, and then looks for another of your linked devices to sync with.

If nobody's online, the changes stay queued locally and sync the moment another device appears.

## Work Offline

You can keep working with no internet. Edits, uploads, new spaces — all fine. When you reconnect, Spacewave figures out what's new on each device and exchanges only the differences.

If you edited the same file on two devices while offline, both versions are kept. The most recent one wins in the viewer, and the earlier one is retained in history so nothing is silently lost.

## Link a New Device

Device linking takes about a minute:

1. Open device linking on a device that's already signed in.
2. A pairing code or QR code appears (valid for 10 minutes).
3. On the new device, enter the code or scan the QR.
4. Both devices confirm.
5. The new device joins your account and starts syncing your spaces.

Once linked, the new device has access to every space. There's no per-space setup to redo.

## Install a Plugin

Plugins add capabilities to a space. Browse the plugin list, install one, and its features show up immediately. Each plugin runs in a sandbox, so it only sees the space it's installed in, not your other spaces or your device.

## Share a Space with Someone Else

Your linked devices share everything automatically. To share a specific space with *another person*, transfer it to an [organization](/docs/users/organizations/organizations). Every member of that org then has access. See [Transfer Space Ownership](/docs/users/organizations/transfer-space-ownership).
